My First Prototype

My Task

For this project, we were to create a proper prototype for a website, and for me the website in question was Gamepress, A site for mobile games with an emphasis on strategy. My focus was on the Pokemon Go portion of the page, since the whole site was a bit big.

Creating a Sitemap

To start, I was instructed to do a case study with about three people and use the information gleaned off of that to make a sitemap to illustrate the general structure of the site. I had trouble finding people to do this project, as I still had yet to really make solid friends (first term nerves and all that). Luckily I was able to get over it and get some results anyhow. This was also when I realized that web design software can be a little pricey. The site I used to make my sitemap was good; it made it easy to line things up and make a simple looking map. However, they also had some high costs on services. Thankfully, I was okay to buy it, but it made me realize that Web Designers get paid so well for a reason, apparently.

Wireframes and I: A Rough Relationship

Next it was time to come up with some ideas for wireframes. This wasn't too bad, as I could do it on paper. This meant I could just draw it up when inspiration hit. This would have been nice, had inspiration decided to hit a bit more often than it did. This lead to me spending many days just trying to figure out what to put down. Eventually, I ended up filling the page with ideas that only slightly iterated from each other. This lead into the final prototype of the new Gamepress, which I have to say came out pretty well for my first shot at UX design. I think I was most successful at coming up with creative ideas, but one thing I needed then (and could always use now) is to figure out a way to have these ideas without wasting so much time.
